Overview

Purpose

River Rafting is a recreational outdoor activity and a competitive sport in which our purpose is to navigate a river or other body of water using raft. This is considered an extreme sport and highly challenging with a little risk attached. It is considered one the most outdoor activities that requires teamwork and dealing with risk as well. There are several benefits that is associated with river rafting which include relaxation, greater physical fitness and help you to appreciate nature. We aspire to give our customers a memorable experience of a lifetime, happiness through our services and bring them closer to nature.

Audience

River Rafting is a recreational activity is based on more physically working out and dealing with some amount of risk as well. This activity is mostly recommended for young people between the ages of 18-45 from all backgrounds, with different kind of beliefs. Both male and female are welcome to have a taste of this unforgettable experience, which is most likely to happen once in a lifetime. People from all kind of educational and professional backgrounds, groups and organizations are welcome to participate in this experience of a lifetime. We also encourage all people of all age to be part of this once the need arises.

Poppins family font is a geometric sans-serif with a fairly high x-height. It is one of the popular design tool for building website. It comes with very good pair for many fonts, looks good on the browser and easy to read as well.

Paragraph Font: Manrope

Manrope family fonts are fonts can be used every purpose and all projects. The font looks great and amazing which make it easy to read on a browser.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
